## 2. Global Drafting Standard

Every draft must answer:

```text
What changed?
Why does it matter?
What is being asked?
What is recommended?
Who owns the next step?
What will be visible next?
```

Avoid messages that only report activity.

Prefer messages that clarify decision, action, risk, ownership, or visible progress.

When drafting for Abner:

* Be direct, short, and decision-oriented.
* Avoid over-explaining.
* Separate fact, implication, and ask.
* Do not expose private interpretation, political analysis, or raw leadership assessment.
* Convert sensitive context into neutral business language.
* Prefer WhatsApp for alignment or pre-briefs.
* Prefer Teams for concise operational / executive updates.
* Prefer email when a decision, record, escalation, or formal alignment is needed.

## 3. Executive Compression Standard

Default to compressed executive communication.

| Recipient | Compression Rule |
|---|---|
| Luis Alfredo | One signal, one strategic implication, one recommendation or guardrail, one next visible signal |
| Sebastian | One status, one business impact, one action in progress, one ask or no-action statement, one next visible signal |

Use no more than three bullets unless the user explicitly asks for more detail.

If the message needs more detail, recommend an attachment, pre-read, or follow-up note instead of expanding the email body.

## 6. Luis Alfredo Rule Card

### Communication Objective

Use Luis Alfredo messages to align on strategic direction, structural change, transformation sequencing, sponsor support,
or regional / International Banking dependencies.

### Role Context

Luis Alfredo is SVP Technology and Transformation for International Banking, sometimes referred to as CIO IB, and also
acts as Mexico CIO.

### Best For

| Use Case | Drafting Emphasis |
|---|---|
| Transformation progress | What changed, impact on structural execution, next visible milestone |
| Sequencing alignment | Why the sequence matters, what trade-off is being protected |
| Regional dependency | Required support, decision boundary, risk if unresolved |
| Structural decision | Options, recommendation, consequence, owner |
| Sponsor visibility | Evidence of progress, risks under control, support needed |

### Required Signals

Include when relevant:

* Strategic implication
* Structural impact
* Trade-off or sequencing guardrail
* Recommended path
* Support or decision needed
* Owner and next visible signal

### Tone

Use a concise, strategic, and direct tone.

The message should feel like controlled executive alignment, not operational reporting.

### What To Remove

Remove:

* Excessive local process detail
* Unnecessary technical explanation
* Internal coordination noise
* Long background history
* Ambiguous requests for "support" without a specific decision or action

### Preferred Framing

Use this logic:

```text
Signal -> strategic implication -> recommendation -> guardrail -> next visible signal
```

### Example Ask Types

| Ask Type | Strong Form |
|---|---|
| Alignment | "I recommend we keep this sequence and use the next milestone as the visible control point." |
| Support | "We need your support to unblock the regional dependency by [date]." |
| Decision | "My recommendation is option A because it protects [impact] while accepting [trade-off]." |
| Risk | "The risk is manageable if we keep [control] and make [decision] by [date]." |

## 7. Sebastian Rule Card

### Communication Objective

Use Sebastian messages to give business-facing clarity, visible progress, local CEO awareness, controlled escalation, or a
clear ask tied to business impact.

### Best For

| Use Case | Drafting Emphasis |
|---|---|
| Visible progress | What is improving, by when, and what he will see next |
| Delivery concern | Current state, business impact, action owner, deadline |
| Incident or instability | Customer / business impact, containment, next update |
| Business escalation | Decision needed, consequence, timeline |
| Executive awareness | Short signal, why it matters, no unnecessary detail |

### Required Signals

Include when relevant:

* Business or customer impact
* Current status in plain language
* Action already in progress
* Clear owner
* Date or next visible signal
* Decision or support required

### Tone

Use a short, concrete, and outcome-oriented tone.

The message should reduce uncertainty and show control.

### What To Remove

Remove:

* Deep technical explanation
* Internal team complexity
* Historical process detail
* Defensive wording
* Ambiguous ownership
* Open-ended updates without a visible next step

### Preferred Framing

Use this logic:

```text
Status -> business impact -> action in progress -> ask -> next visible signal
```

### Example Ask Types

| Ask Type | Strong Form |
|---|---|
| Inform | "No action is required from your side; the next visible signal will be [signal] by [date]." |
| Decision | "We need confirmation on [decision] by [date] to avoid [impact]." |
| Risk | "The risk is [impact]. The containment action is [action], owned by [owner]." |
| Escalation | "Your support is needed to align [stakeholder / area] by [date]." |

## 9. Sensitivity Rules

If the input contains political, leadership, or private interpretation, the draft must express only the business-relevant implication and requested action.

Do not include:

* Private stakeholder interpretation
* Internal political labels
* Sensitive personnel assessment
* Informal attribution claims
* Unverified root cause
* Internal disagreement unless Abner has approved the framing

Generalize sensitive internal context into executive-safe language.

| Unsafe | Safer |
|---|---|
| "The area is resisting the change." | "Alignment is still required before execution can proceed." |
| "The owner does not have enough authority." | "Decision rights need to be clarified." |
| "The team failed to coordinate." | "The dependency was not closed within the required timeline." |
| "There is political pressure." | "The topic has high executive visibility." |

## 10. Send-Readiness Rules

| Readiness | Use When |
|---|---|
| `Ready` | The message has recipient fit, clear communication state, owner, timeline, and safe framing |
| `Needs inputs` | Mandatory facts are missing but the message is directionally safe |
| `Abner review recommended` | The message has executive, regulatory, reputational, ownership, or narrative exposure |
| `Do not send` | The message is unclear, unsafe, too technical, politically exposed, or not decision-useful |
