# Incident Communication Reset - Case

Version: v1.0

---

## Date

2026-05

---

## Signals

* Incident ownership was split across engineering and SR, creating confusion and duplicated effort.
* Some P4 incidents behaved like higher-criticality events but lacked visibility for the teams handling more severe incidents.
* Executive pressure increased through repeated complaints and informal-channel escalation.
* A single Teams channel created a clearer source of truth for Technology and Business.
* Business areas responded positively to greater transparency and friendlier incident language.
* A simplified, non-technical incident management process diagram helped business stakeholders understand activation, prioritization, response, and engineering escalation.
* Sebastián gave unusually positive feedback in a 1:1 after seeing the simplified process view.

---

## Structural Findings

* Incident severity labels are insufficient when ownership and visibility are fragmented.
* A single source of truth reduces coordination cost across Technology and Business.
* Business-friendly incident language improves trust without exposing internal complexity.
* AI-assisted drafting can raise communication quality for SR teams under pressure.
* Distilled process artifacts can create alignment before formal process documentation is complete.

---

## Political / Behavioral Insights

* Informal-channel pressure weakens when the formal channel creates visible value.
* Resistance to a new operating structure can decline once other stakeholders validate it.
* Deriving questions back to the official channel is part of enforcing the system.
* Executive resistance can soften when a structural process is translated into a simple business-readable operating model.
* Sebastián responds better to visible structure and executive-level clarity than to raw operational detail.

---

## Decision

* Critical incident communication should happen through one official Teams channel.
* Parallel channels may receive questions, but answers should redirect to the official channel.
* Business stakeholders should receive simplified process views for major operating resets.
