# Technology Organization Simplification - Case

Version: v1.0

---

## Date

2026-05

---

## Signals

* Luis Alfredo's Peru visit increased executive pressure and support for structural Technology changes.
* International Banking alignment reinforced the need to simplify the organization.
* A directional reduction target of roughly 150 people emerged from the organizational diagnosis.
* Local diagnosis indicates hidden coordination layers and roles that mainly pass information between teams.
* Raul Hugo helped provide visibility into Engineering structure and accountability gaps.
* Sebastián showed less resistance and more openness after greater stability, visibility, and International Banking alignment.
* An International Banking team translated the directional reduction target into an immediate list request before the local sequencing context was clear.

---

## Structural Findings

* Formal organization charts can hide accountability dilution.
* Middle-management and coordination layers can become information-relay structures rather than ownership structures.
* Large reductions should be designed as operating-model simplification, not isolated headcount removal.
* Sequencing is required to avoid removing critical continuity capacity before the new operating model can absorb the work.

---

## Political / Behavioral Insights

* International Banking support can create political cover for changes that are difficult to push locally.
* Political cover can also create over-acceleration if support teams act without local context.
* Sebastián's resistance can soften when Technology appears stable, visible, and externally validated.
* Improved stakeholder openness should be treated as an operating window that requires reinforcement through evidence.

---

## Decision

* Workforce reduction targets should be treated as directional mandates.
* Name-level reduction lists require role-function evidence, dependency analysis, continuity assessment, and HR/legal sequencing.
* Personal OS should capture the reusable operating pattern and decision guardrails, not operational headcount lists.
