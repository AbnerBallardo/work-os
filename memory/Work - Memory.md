## Decision Patterns

* [pattern] Short-term pressure systematically biases decisions toward suboptimal quick fixes
* [pattern] Structural solutions require repeated justification under escalating pressure
* [pattern] Alignment must precede execution to avoid resistance and rework
* [pattern] Visible progress signals reduce escalation regardless of actual system state
* [pattern] Lack of structure increases coordination cost and execution friction
* [pattern] Trusted operators with political wear require narrative protection to keep their capability usable
* [pattern] Regionalized functions require explicit authority mechanisms or they drift into advisory detail work
* [pattern] Formal title conversion depends on visible wins, narrative control, and executive validation, not only actual scope performed
* [pattern] Firefighter leaders solve urgent issues but do not automatically create structural operating-model change
* [pattern] Relationship-management roles can mask missing local capability ownership
* [pattern] Information-relay roles can mask missing accountability ownership inside formal organizational structures
* [pattern] Stakeholders may protect influence or functional accountability even while participating constructively in problem solving
* [pattern] Fragmented incident channels create visibility gaps even when severity taxonomy exists
* [pattern] A single business-facing incident channel reduces confusion by making truth, owner, status, and discovery path visible
* [pattern] Distilled process diagrams help convert technical operating resets into executive alignment
* [pattern] Resistance to new communication structure decreases when stakeholders experience transparency gains
* [pattern] External validation (International Banking) shifts local power balance
* [pattern] International Banking pressure can convert local resistance into openness when stability and visibility are improving
* [pattern] Wider business accountability can make previously ignored technology implications more visible to executive stakeholders
* [pattern] Directional executive targets can be mistranslated by support teams into artificial urgent deliverables
* [pattern] If pressure overrides structure → system degradation accelerates
* [pattern] If narrative diverges from execution → trust erosion begins
* [pattern] Regulatory enforcement often exposes long-standing architectural weaknesses rather than recent execution failures
* [pattern] Late regulatory awareness creates artificial urgency and misaligned accountability
* [pattern] Integration between platforms amplifies hidden system fragility
* [pattern] Under pressure, organizations optimize for visible action over effective outcomes
