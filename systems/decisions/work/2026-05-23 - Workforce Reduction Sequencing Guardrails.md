## Context

Technology transformation has exposed organizational bloat inside coordination and middle-management layers. Some roles function mainly as information relays instead of accountable execution owners.

International Banking alignment supports structural simplification, including a directional reduction target of roughly 150 people. However, converting that target into a name-level list under artificial urgency creates material organizational risk.

Luis Alfredo clarified that the reduction does not require an immediate one-week list; it should be handled as the transformation sequence makes the right moves visible.

---

## Decision

Treat workforce reduction targets as directional operating-model simplification mandates, not immediate list-production requests.

No name-level reduction list should be committed until the following are explicit:

* Role-function diagnosis
* Accountability map
* Dependency and continuity assessment
* Sequencing plan
* Stakeholder alignment
* HR and legal execution path

The Personal OS should store the decision logic and guardrails, not the actual reduction list.

## Alternatives Considered

* Produce the reduction list immediately to satisfy perceived urgency.
* Reject the reduction target as too risky.
* Accept the direction, but sequence execution through evidence, impact assessment, and governance.

---

## Trade-Offs

* Speed vs correctness: immediate list production creates visible action but increases risk of damaging critical capacity.
* Political alignment vs operational safety: International Banking support is valuable, but local execution must control sequencing.
* Simplification vs continuity: removing relay roles is necessary, but critical operational continuity must be protected during transition.

---

## Constraints Applied

* Workforce changes are politically sensitive and partially irreversible.
* Formal org charts do not fully expose accountability dilution.
* International Banking teams may translate directional executive intent into premature artifact requests.
* Technology transformation still requires stable execution during the reduction sequence.

---

## Risks Accepted

* Slower visible progress on the reduction target.
* Continued short-term carrying cost for some low-value roles while evidence is gathered.
* Potential pressure from stakeholders who interpret sequencing as lack of urgency.

---

* Invalidation condition: International Banking changes the strategic target, HR/legal constraints require a different process, or operating-model evidence disproves the assumed relay-role bloat.
